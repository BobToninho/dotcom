Strollo is closing his laptop on yet another boring day writing React code for Zottier. The sun is still up, but not for long and just about to approach the mountain that he can see from his window. Strollo tries to stand from his desk but his legs twitch, remembering him that he sat four hours before and didn't move ever since. Together with the legs, the back hurts too. Strollo finds the pain annoying, so much that he checks the Gmail app to see if the gym subscription is still active. The email is not easy to find and an Instagram notification about his friend Marcus commenting three fire emojis on his latest post pops up. After opening Instagram, Strollo sees stories of a bunch of girls he followed the past week (but they didn't follow back). It seems they are traveling to Finland to ..., an area full of (fiordi) and then move to ... to have some camping days. -Why was I checking the phone in the first place?- Strollo asks himself before shrugging and preparing a hot tea.

It's 16:00 and Strollo has a pair programming session via Zoom with Bardo, a senior engineer at Zottier; they work together since long time so the meeting comes with no pressure for Strollo. The same can't be said for their pair programming sessions in the early days of Strollo. Bardo would ping Strollo on Slack and setup a meeting as everybody else does, then Bardo would show up 15 mintues later, or together with a woman, or not show up at all, or eating a cake. (maybe expand on this) Once, after waiting 20 minutes for Bardo, Strollo stood up from his chair and started screaming and taking hair from his beard. Bardo arrived in the moment Strollo started the screaming. Once back to his chair, Strollo realized he hadn't turned off the video as he did with the microphone; his heart missed a beat or two. Bardo smiled and started the meeting in a rush, (borbottando) against the meeting starting late and ignoring Strollo for the first five minutes. The meeting ended after less than ten minutes after Bardo arrived in order to respect meeting etiquette at Zottier. After many years of working together, Strollo got used to Bardo's behavior but still isn't sure what to think of him. He never gave him any feedback and thinks he never will. It's hard to predict how bad of a reaction a negative feedback form would have. Bardo could get angry at him and follow him at home, or ignore the feedback and keep things as they are, or riot for Strollo to be fired because there is surely something that can be reported about Strollo's work.
